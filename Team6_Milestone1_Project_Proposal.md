## Vision Statement
Example: StudySync is a collaborative web application designed to help small student teams plan study sessions, coordinate availability, and track shared academic goals. The platform supports both synchronous and asynchronous collaboration by combining scheduling tools, task tracking, and progress visualization in one place.

---

## Motivation
Example:  
Many students struggle to coordinate group work across different schedules, time zones, and course loads, often relying on fragmented tools like shared documents, group chats, and calendars. StudySync aims to simplify this process by providing a single, purpose-built platform that makes planning, accountability, and communication more transparent for student teams.

---

## Risks to Project Completion
Example:  
- **Scheduling and Coordination Challenges:** Team members are in different time zones, which may limit synchronous development time.  
- **Feature Scope Creep:** There is a risk of trying to implement too many collaboration features within the semester timeline.  
- **Learning Curve with Full-Stack Integration:** Some team members have limited experience connecting frontend interfaces with backend APIs and databases.

---

## Mitigation Strategy
Example:  
- **Availability Alignment:** The team will rely on asynchronous communication tools and keep synchronous meetings focused and time-boxed.  
- **Minimum Viable Product First:** The initial goal will be a basic, functional MVP focusing on scheduling and task tracking before adding enhancements.  
- **Early Prototyping:** Backend routes and database schemas will be implemented early to reduce integration risk later in the project.

---

## Development Method
Example:  
The team will follow an Agile, sprint-based approach with weekly planning meetings, short development iterations, and continuous integration. Tasks will be broken into small, testable components, allowing the project to adapt based on progress and challenges.

---

## Development Steps
Example: 
1. Define core user stories and functional requirements.  
2. Design database schema for users, groups, tasks, and availability.  
3. Implement backend API endpoints for scheduling and task management.  
4. Build frontend views for calendars, task lists, and progress summaries.  
5. Integrate frontend and backend components.  
6. Conduct testing and prepare final presentation and documentation.

---

## Project Tracking
Example: 
The team will use Trello to track tasks, sprint progress, and blockers. Tasks will be labeled by priority and assigned to individual team members to maintain accountability.
